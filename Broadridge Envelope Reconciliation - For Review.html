Items for review

Wastage — contractual vs. operational

The contract caps the wastage charge at 5% (original, through Dec 2023) and 2% (Amendment No. 1, Jan 2024+). Broadridge has separately confirmed that actual operational wastage runs 10–15%.

Contract max wastage (5%/2% blended)690,734 envelopes
Operational wastage at 10%1,846,994 envelopes
Operational wastage at 15%2,770,492 envelopes
Excess beyond contract allowance1,156,260–2,079,758 envelopes

Per Section 4, excess wastage beyond the contractual rate is Broadridge’s responsibility. We would like to confirm the current operational wastage rate and how it is reflected in invoicing.

Excess inventory

After accounting for contractual wastage, post-settlement purchases exceed adjusted usage by 1,878,838 envelopes. At the trailing 12-month average usage of 284,200/month, this represents approximately 6.6 months of buffer stock.

Broadridge has stated a policy of maintaining 2–3 months of envelope supply (Brandon Koebel, Nov 2022). Section 8 requires Broadridge to be “responsible for procuring and maintaining sufficient quantity of Materials, based on average volumes.”

We would like to understand the current inventory position and whether ordering volumes are being adjusted to align with declining usage trends.

Outstanding items for Broadridge confirmation

  1. Data validation: Please review the purchased and used totals in this report and confirm they align with Broadridge’s records.
  2. Wastage rate applied: What wastage rate is currently being applied to envelope charges? The contract specifies 5% (original) / 2% (amendment) for generic envelope stock. Operational wastage has been confirmed at 10–15% — please clarify whether the invoiced rate reflects the contractual rate or the operational rate.
  3. Unit rate breakdown: Please provide a sample breakdown of the invoiced unit rate for envelopes showing the vendor price, wastage component, and margin (if applicable) for a recent month.
  4. Inventory position: Please provide the current envelope inventory levels and confirm whether ordering volumes are being adjusted to reflect the decline in average monthly usage.

Contract terms — envelope materials

Original Contract — Section 4, Compensation

GTO Print and Mail Services Schedule, effective January 1, 2019.

“Materials (such as paper, envelopes, and inserts) and postage, presort and insert related fees are not included in the Annual Fee and will be charged separately. Materials are billed at cost plus wastage for generic stock. Specifically, the wastage charge is 10% for any generic paper stock and 5% for generic envelope stock. For generic stock, the unit rate will be billed based on usage. For Client specific stock, the unit rate will be based on receipt of such stock.”

Amendment No. 1 — Section 4, Compensation (replaces original)

GTO Print and Mail Services Schedule Amendment No. 1, effective January 1, 2024. Term extended through December 31, 2028.

“Materials are billed at inventory cost plus 10% margin. Inventory cost means for (i) Client specific inventory: vendor price; and (ii) generic inventory: vendor price plus wastage as follows: 10% for continuous form, 3% for cutsheet, and 2% for envelopes. For generic stock, the unit rate will be billed based on usage. For Client specific stock, the unit rate will be based on receipt of such stock.”

Original Contract — Section 8, Materials

“Client will specify the materials (paper, inserts and envelope) (“Materials”) to be used; provided, however, that materials specified must conform to Broadridge print and insert equipment specifications. Broadridge shall be responsible for procuring and maintaining sufficient quantity of Materials, based on average volumes, except where Client is responsible for Materials as specified in Section 3.”
